Suggestions for giving feedback

  • Play the game!
  • Describe your experience
    • Describe what you did, what you thought while you were doing it, and what you were trying to do.
    • Avoid making judgments or suggestions at this point, the creator can come to their own conclusions based on their design goals.
  • Start by mentioning something you liked!
    • Remember that sharing work can be a vulnerable experience : )
  • Wait to make for suggestions until the creator asks for them, or first ask: "Is it okay for me to make a suggestion".
  • Consider asking first:
    • Is there anything specific you are looking for feedback on?
      • It's very possible that the person you're giving feedback for is looking for something specific, or that they didn't have time to get to something.

Suggestions for receiving feedback

  • Watch and listen
    • You will likely get the best information by simply watching someone play your game without your help.
    • Exception: If your game is not yet in a place where someone can play without guidance, it's okay to help the player get to the part that you want feedback about. Just remember that someday you won't be there.
  • Ask them to describe their experience (see previous section)
    • You already saw what they did, but it might be helpful to uncover what they were thinking while they were doing it.
  • Ask for specific feedback as it relates to a specific aspect of your game or in connection to one of your specific design goals.
  • Give content warnings
    • If your game deals with sensitive subjects, let your player know beforehand. Allow your playtester to decline playing for this reason if needed

Core takeaways

  • Feedback resulting from playtesting can often be contradictory. Different people will want different things.
  • Prioritize what you watch people do, over what they say. People often have trouble giving negative feedback, and/or difficulty with putting their feedback into words, but if someone gets stuck while they're playing...
  • Your job as a designer is to take these contradictions and incorporate them back into your work to provide the experience you want to achieve. You can't do it all!
