My take on what a game design class should include:

  • Students should play and discuss games as part of class.
  • Students should develop skills that can be applied to game development regardless of current in-vogue technologies.
  • I should present non-dominant narratives about games, with a focus on equity and accessibility. You can get dominant narratives about games anywhere!
  • I should provide professional perspectives for a variety of student goals such as:
    • wanting to work at a large game company
    • wanting to freelance or start your own game studio
    • interest in entering the fine art world
    • and anything else related to "games" that students let me know about!
  • I should provide some insight into how to avoid being exploited within the industry.

A final thought on this class:

We aren't going to spend much time defining what a "game" is.

I think it is more useful to ask ourselves: "Why do I want to call this specific thing I'm making a game?"

You're the creator! You can call what you do whatever you want, we should just start thinking about the effect that calling it a "game" or "art" or an "experience" will have on how an audience perceives it.
